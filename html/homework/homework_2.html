Animals

Bear

Bears are large mammals found in various habitats across the Northern Hemisphere. They are powerful predators and omnivores, known for their strength, intelligence, and ability to adapt to different environments. Bears play a crucial role in their ecosystems as seed dispersers and predators.

  • Weight: 300-750 kg (depending on species)
  • Length: 1.5-3 meters
  • Main Food Sources: Fish, berries, vegetation, small mammals
  • Life Expectancy: 20-40 years

Lion

Lions are apex predators and the second-largest cats in the world, native to Africa and India. Known for their majestic manes and powerful roars, they live in social groups called prides. Lions are skilled hunters and symbols of strength and courage across many cultures.

  • Weight: 150-250 kg
  • Length: 1.7-2.5 meters (body)
  • Main Food Sources: Zebras, antelopes, wildebeest, small mammals
  • Life Expectancy: 10-14 years (wild), up to 20 years (captivity)

Shark

Sharks are ancient marine predators that have existed for over 400 million years. They are highly adapted to ocean life with streamlined bodies and exceptional sensory abilities. Most sharks are carnivorous and play a vital role in maintaining ocean ecosystem balance.

  • Weight: 50-2,000+ kg (varies by species)
  • Length: 1-18 meters (varies by species)
  • Main Food Sources: Fish, smaller sharks, marine mammals, seals
  • Life Expectancy: 20-70 years (depends on species)
